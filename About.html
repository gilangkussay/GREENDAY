Our Story & Achievements

Over the years, Green Day has won five Grammy Awards, including Best Rock Album, and sold over 75 million records globally. Their album "American Idiot" was adapted into a hit Broadway musical, further cementing their legacy.

Band History

1986: Formation of Green Day

Green Day was formed in Berkeley, California, by Billie Joe Armstrong and Mike Dirnt. Initially, the band was called "Sweet Children" before changing its name to Green Day.

1990: Release of the First Album

Green Day released their debut studio album, "39/Smooth," which caught the attention of punk rock fans. The album reflected the raw energy and spirit of punk.

1994: Success of Dookie

Their second album, "Dookie," was released and became one of the best-selling punk rock albums of all time. Songs like "Basket Case" and "When I Come Around" made them internationally famous.

2004: American Idiot

"American Idiot" was launched as a response to political and social discontent. The album won a Grammy Award and became one of Green Day's most iconic works.

2009: 21st Century Breakdown

Green Day continued the tradition of concept albums with "21st Century Breakdown," highlighting recent events and telling the story of a young man's journey through a chaotic world.

2020: Father of All Motherfuckers

Their latest album, "Father of All Motherfuckers," was released in 2020, showing their musical evolution with a more modern and diverse sound.
